To Air Is Human

We are on a mission to help you breathe easy.

Everyone has the right to breathe clean air.
And yet, we don’t.

Air pollution is a leading cause of death worldwide. A health crisis of this magnitude should inspire global change — and yet, it doesn’t. Catastrophic headlines that incite a strong public reaction are too often followed by inaction.

ClimateOrb exists to make a difference for you, and the world:

95% of the world breathes heavily polluted air.

Indoor air can be 2 to 5 times more polluted
than the worst outdoor air.

7 million people die of air pollution every year.

We believe data is the solution, for you and the world.

With accurate information to inspire solutions, progress and change can help clear the air. Powered by our network of indoor air sensors, we can leverage data on a global scale to track and end air pollution.

We bring the data to you for better health
We bring the data to researchers and governments for global change.
Together, we can map a way forward.

At ClimateOrb, we integrate the most advanced air sensing to analyze air quality and environmental factors so you have the best information possible to protect yourself, and the world. Our proprietary cloud-based blockchain model provides our users with accurate, secured data to ensure that your indoor air is helping you enjoy a healthier, better life. Learn more about our state-of-the-art technology here.

Ultimately, we want to foster a healthy environment for a healthy you.

Using technology to breathe human.

Joshua Anthony

AERIAL ROBOTICS ENGINEER

I am an Aeronautical Engineer by training, having completed my masters’ degree in 2019. Along side my work at ClimateOrb I am working towards my PhD in Experiment Environmental Fluid Mechanics with a focus on pollutant dispersion in urban environments and urban heat islands. In my role as a Mechanical Engineer I have been involved in the mechanical design of the PriDa, leaf, and JamPac air quality sensors for the company, with PRiDA being our market ready product. ClimateOrb’s focus has been on indoor air quality for the most part up to this point. The next stage of my work is using UAV technologies, using my experience from three previous projects during my degree and experience in this field, to provide outdoor air quality measurements to help expand the platform further to inform people of their environment. As October 2020, I have undertaken the role of UK co-ordinator where I manage our activities and product interests in the UK.
